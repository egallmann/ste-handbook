# Engineering as Decision-Making

This chapter explains software and systems engineering as the progressive reduction of design space under constraints. It positions architectural work as explicit decision-making whose outcomes should be traceable, revisitable, and checkable.

## Why this matters

If engineering is only described as code or tickets, intent disappears. STE assumes decisions are first-class inputs to later validation and governance.

## Planned coverage

- Design space, constraints, and trade-offs
- Explicit versus implicit decisions
- Decision records as durable engineering artifacts

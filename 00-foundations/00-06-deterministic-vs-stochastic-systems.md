# Deterministic versus Stochastic Systems

This chapter contrasts components whose behavior is effectively predictable under stated assumptions from those that are inherently variable (for example, stochastic models or non-deterministic execution). It clarifies vocabulary for later discussion of assessment and agents.

## Why this matters

STE emphasizes deterministic assessment where possible; stochastic elements require explicit framing. Readers need a shared vocabulary to avoid category errors.

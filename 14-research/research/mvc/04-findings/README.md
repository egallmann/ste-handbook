# MVC Findings

## The Problem

This directory records published MVC findings. Findings are research evidence interpretations; they are not STE authority.

## The Reframe

Findings must preserve positive, negative, inconclusive, and superseded outcomes so the research record does not optimize for confirmation.

## The Model

| Classification | Meaning |
|----------------|---------|
| Published Findings | Findings published from a declared research configuration and publication version. |
| Negative Findings | Findings that challenge a candidate theory, hypothesis, method, or expected effect. |
| Inconclusive Findings | Findings that do not support a strong interpretation because the evidence or instrument is insufficient. |
| Superseded Findings | Findings replaced or narrowed by later research while remaining discoverable. |

## The Implications

- Negative findings must be retained.
- Inconclusive findings must be retained.
- Superseded findings must remain discoverable.
- Positive findings receive no special authority.
- Findings should identify the research configuration and publication version they derive from.

## Relationship to STE system

Findings may inform promotion proposals but do not replace ADRs, contracts, invariants, benchmarks, or Kernel admission.

## Summary

- Findings are research interpretations.
- Negative and inconclusive findings are retained.
- Superseded findings remain discoverable.
- No MVC findings are published in this scaffold yet.

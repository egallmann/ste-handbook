# MVC Open Questions

## The Problem

Open questions are required living research artifacts. They prevent premature closure around favored explanations and preserve uncertainty across study generations.

## The Reframe

The MVC research program keeps uncertainty explicit. Questions may remain open across multiple experiment generations and may be retired only when findings, reproductions, or promotion reviews make the reason clear.

## The Model

Current open questions:

- Which parts of the representation ceiling theory are substrate effects, assembly effects, or reasoner effects?
- What measurement can distinguish representation quality from context assembly quality?
- When does additional context become harmful rather than merely unnecessary?
- What evidence would distinguish a weak benchmark from a weak candidate theory?
- Which MVC findings, if reproduced, would justify promotion review for contracts or governance surfaces?
- How should candidate equations evolve as more findings and reproductions accumulate?
- How should candidate context artifacts map to MVC-S fixture outputs without implying production MVC-M?
- Which hosted or rubric validation path is sufficient to move beyond local signal?
- What live HSCA observation volume is enough to support substrate completeness claims?
- Which repeated local results are stable enough to justify stronger evidence claims?
- Which candidate equation variables are stable enough to keep, rename, or retire?
- Which interaction terms dominate additive representation effects?
- Which inferred search dimensions require new executable policy authority before use?
- When are assembly configurations meaningfully equivalent across substrates, and when is non-equivalence the honest result?
- Can context preflight predict whether a study condition is viable, degraded, or invalid before evaluation?

Required categories:

Each research program should track:

- unvalidated assumptions,
- competing explanations,
- candidate theories,
- unresolved validity concerns,
- future evidence needs.

Progress criteria:

- live blinded HSCA observations at the active evidence boundary,
- hosted or rubric validation path available,
- adjudication protocol drafted,
- candidate context to MVC-S fixture mapping defined,
- repeated local runs show stable candidate discrimination,
- evolution operators defined and tested under governed fitness weighting.
- configuration identity, equivalence, and drift are captured consistently enough for reproduction.

Potential falsification remains explicit:

- reasoners reliably recover never-encoded relationships, constraints, alternatives, and assumptions from outcomes alone,
- degraded representations perform as well as structurally richer representations under controlled tasks,
- improvements track reasoner capability only and not representation quality.

## The Implications

Open questions prevent findings from being treated as closure when competing explanations or validity concerns remain.

## Relationship to STE system

Open questions are research artifacts. They may motivate future studies or promotion reviews, but they do not change STE authority surfaces.

## Summary

- Open questions are required living artifacts.
- Questions may remain open across multiple generations.
- Progress criteria and falsification targets remain visible.
- Explicit uncertainty prevents premature closure.

# MVC Thesis Publications

The reasoning-ceiling illustration lives on the [MVC research program](../README.md) landing page (`01-thesis/images/ste-reasoning-ceiling-hero.png`). It is a program overview image, not a modification to the thesis publication. This page covers thesis publication lineage only.

## The Problem

This directory publishes thesis-level MVC research artifacts. Thesis publications state candidate theories and conceptual claims. They do not define normative STE contracts.

## The Reframe

Thesis publications are historical research records. They should evolve through visible publication lineage rather than silent replacement.

## The Model

- Published research artifacts are historical records.
- Minor corrections may be applied in place.
- Substantive revisions create a new publication version.
- Prior versions remain discoverable and referenceable.
- Findings should identify the publication version they were derived from.

Preferred naming for substantive versions:

- `mvc-thesis-v1.md`
- `mvc-thesis-v2.md`

The published thesis is [mvc-representation-ceiling-thesis.md](mvc-representation-ceiling-thesis.md). The program overview image on the landing page summarizes the research program around the thesis: reasoning quality is bounded by the structural quality of represented and assembled context, not by reasoning alone. Substantive revisions should create a new versioned publication file (for example `mvc-thesis-v2.md`) while keeping prior versions discoverable.

## The Implications

Findings should identify the thesis publication version they derive from.

## Relationship to STE system

Thesis publications are research artifacts. They do not replace ADRs, contracts, or invariants.

## Summary

- Thesis publications preserve candidate theories.
- Substantive revisions create new publication versions.
- Prior versions remain discoverable.
